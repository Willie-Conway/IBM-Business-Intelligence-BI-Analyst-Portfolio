

## **Key Variables Identified in the Superstore Sales Dataset**

After reviewing the Superstore Sales dataset, several key variables stand out as most relevant for analyzing sales performance and supporting business decision-making. These variables fall into four main categories: **sales performance**, **customer characteristics**, **product information**, and **geographic data**.

---

### **1. Sales & Financial Metrics (Most Critical)**

These variables directly measure business performance and profitability:

* **Sales** – Total revenue generated from orders; the primary metric for evaluating performance.
* **Profit** – Indicates profitability and helps identify high- and low-margin products or regions.
* **Quantity** – Number of units sold; useful for demand analysis and inventory planning.
* **Discount** – Helps assess the impact of promotions on sales and profit.

**Why they matter:**
These metrics allow the business to track revenue growth, profitability trends, and the effectiveness of pricing and discount strategies.

---

### **2. Time-Based Variables**

These variables help identify trends and seasonality:

* **Order Date** – Used to analyze sales trends over time (monthly, quarterly, yearly).
* **Ship Date** – Useful for evaluating fulfillment efficiency and delivery performance.

**Why they matter:**
Time-based analysis helps identify peak sales periods, seasonal demand patterns, and long-term growth trends.

---

### **3. Customer & Segment Data**

These variables describe who is generating the revenue:

* **Customer Segment** (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office)
* **Customer ID / Customer Name**

**Why they matter:**
They help identify the most valuable customer segments and tailor marketing, pricing, and retention strategies accordingly.

---

### **4. Product & Category Information**

These variables describe what is being sold:

* **Product Category** (Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology)
* **Sub-Category**
* **Product Name**

**Why they matter:**
They allow analysis of product performance, identification of best-selling items, and optimization of inventory and product focus.

---

### **5. Geographic Data**

These variables show where sales occur:

* **Region**
* **State**
* **City**
* **Country**

**Why they matter:**
Geographic analysis supports regional strategy decisions such as targeted marketing, expansion opportunities, and resource allocation.

---

## **Most Relevant Data Types for the Business Scenario**

For Alex’s BI use case, the most relevant data types are:

* **Numerical data**: Sales, Profit, Quantity, Discount
* **Categorical data**: Region, Segment, Category, Sub-Category
* **Temporal data**: Order Date, Ship Date
* **Geographic data**: Region, State, City

---

## **Business Value**

These variables collectively enable:

* Identification of high-performing products and regions
* Evaluation of customer segment profitability
* Detection of trends and seasonality
* Data-driven decisions for marketing, inventory, and growth strategies
