

# **Chosen Visualization Types and Rationale**

To represent key metrics and trends in the Superstore Sales dataset, the following basic visualizations were created:

### **1. Line Chart – Sales Over Time**

* **Why this visualization:**
  A line chart is ideal for time-series data because it clearly shows trends, patterns, and seasonality.
* **What it represents:**
  Total sales aggregated by order date.
* **Key insight:**
  Sales display noticeable seasonal peaks, particularly toward the end of each year, suggesting increased demand during holiday periods.

---

### **2. Bar Chart – Sales by Product Category**

* **Why this visualization:**
  Bar charts are effective for comparing discrete categories side by side.
* **What it represents:**
  Total sales across major product categories (Technology, Furniture, Office Supplies).
* **Key insight:**
  The Technology category generates the highest total sales, indicating it is the strongest revenue driver for the business.

---

### **3. Pie Chart – Sales Distribution by Region**

* **Why this visualization:**
  A pie chart helps visualize proportional contributions to a total.
* **What it represents:**
  The share of total sales contributed by each region.
* **Key insight:**
  The West and East regions account for the largest portion of total sales, while Central and South contribute smaller shares.

---

## **How These Visualizations Improve Understanding**

These visualizations help in understanding the data by:

* Revealing **sales trends over time**, which supports forecasting and seasonal planning
* Identifying **top-performing categories**, helping prioritize inventory and marketing efforts
* Highlighting **regional sales disparities**, which inform geographic strategy and resource allocation

Together, they provide a high-level overview of business performance while guiding deeper analysis.

---

## **Evaluation and Possible Improvements**

### **Effectiveness**

* Clear titles, labels, and legends ensure the visualizations are easy to interpret
* Each chart communicates a single, focused insight
* The visuals support data-driven decision-making for both technical and non-technical stakeholders

### **Potential Improvements**

* Replace the pie chart with a bar chart for easier regional comparison
* Add profit metrics alongside sales to evaluate profitability, not just revenue
* Introduce interactive filters (e.g., year, region, category) to allow users to explore the data dynamically

---

### **Conclusion**

The selected basic visualizations effectively communicate key metrics and trends in the dataset. They provide a strong foundation for deeper analysis and set the stage for advanced visualizations and interactive dashboards in later steps.
